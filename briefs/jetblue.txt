The Flying Blue iconography guidelines establish a framework for creating imagery that offers a unique, unexpected point of view. These guidelines are built on several key creative pillars:

  * **Immersion:** Images should make the viewer feel as though they are experiencing the moment personally, capturing the presence of program members.
  * **Personality:** Imagery should provide a fresh perspective, actively avoiding conventional, "cliché," or "postcard-style" visuals.
  * **Detail:** Photographers should use evocative close-ups to highlight specific aspects of the program, such as luxury or attention, rather than relying on generic views.
  * **Geometry:** Compositions must be structured, readable, and well-organized to communicate that the program is seamless and easy to use.
  * **Breathing:** To encourage a sense of travel and escape, images should feature clean, minimalist, and open compositions, preferably with a clear horizon and blue sky.
  * **Spontaneity:** Visuals should appear natural, capturing real-time moments. Posed portraits, artificial lighting, staged smiles, and subjects looking directly at the camera should be avoided.

**Prohibited Content and Restrictions**
To maintain brand standards, the following elements must be excluded from imagery:

  * **Behaviors:** Alcohol, drugs, cigarettes, and overt displays of affection like mouth kissing are prohibited. Romantic interactions should be subtle, such as kind glances or hands touching.
  * **Attire and Nudity:** Nudity, bathing suits, and exposed body parts such as legs above the knees, bellies, or bare feet are not permitted.
  * **Objects and Branding:** The use of cars, visible brand logos, religious symbols, and commercial signage (such as for bars or restaurants) is forbidden.
  * **Competitors:** Imagery must not feature visible modes of transport belonging to competitors.
