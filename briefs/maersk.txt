The Maersk imagery guidelines emphasize moving away from business-to-business (B2B) and toward a human-to-human (H2H) style to communicate effectively and consistently.

Key Imagery Principles:

  * **Style and Authenticity**
      * Use relatable and authentic pictures rather than B2B imagery.
      * Images should be less B2B and more H2H.
      * Photos are an integral part of the visual identity and must be used consistently.
      * Authenticity and imperfection are key for relatability; too-perfect images are rejected as unbelievable.
  * **Capture the Moment (Candid vs. Staged)**
      * Search for the right moment, a slice-of-life situation is always relatable.
      * Candid photography is the preferred method.
      * Avoid looking straight into the camera, overly friendly (fake) smiles, and staged expressions.
      * The subject should have character and tell a story.
      * To keep photography real, use real people, employees, or customers.
      * The moment captured should convey a feeling or human sentiment, even if it's a still life.
  * **Visual Lensing and Editing**
      * Visual filters/FX should be limited to retain authenticity and a natural look.
      * Use correct tones, soft with a tinge of blues.
      * Natural lighting is preferred.
      * Avoid heavily color-graded, photoshopped, retouched, or manipulated images.
      * Avoid excessive motion blur, high contrast, double exposure, 2D/3D graphics, and artificial lighting.
  * **Other Considerations**
      * Assets (equipment) can be shown only if they narrate a story, not as a focused piece of equipment.
      * The Maersk logo, tagline, and legal line must be included in communication, except for static social posts where the logo is the profile picture.
