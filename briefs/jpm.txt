This summary outlines the photography guidelines for J.P. Morgan Asset Management.

**Brand Purpose and Style**
Photography is used to humanize the brand, showcase expertise, and reflect the firm's global diversity. Images should tell a story and create a connection with the viewer.

  * **General Aesthetic:** Utilize natural light and ample white space to maintain a clean, crisp, and professional look. Images should be authentic, avoiding harsh angles and overly manicured compositions.
  * **Tonality:** Use cooler tones for broader financial themes and insights, and warmer tones for topics requiring human connection, such as retirement or college planning.
  * **Diversity:** Imagery must reflect the organization's global footprint and cultural diversity.

**Categories of Photography**

  * **Global Intelligence:** Focuses on owned assets, such as industrial settings, using natural light, vivid color, and an architectural approach to composition to convey scale.
  * **People Photography:** Focuses on natural, "in the moment" activities to avoid posed, staged, or art-directed looks. Tight crops are utilized to foster a sense of connection and authenticity.
  * **Textural Photography:** Employs tight, architectural, and simple crops derived from Global Intelligence imagery to support targeted, specific messaging.

**Captions and Misuse**

  * **Captions:** When displaying owned assets, use captions in non-descript areas to provide context such as facts, figures, or location.
  * **Prohibitions:** Avoid cliched subject matter, complex or busy imagery, unnatural color effects, and subjects looking directly at the camera. Imagery should never feel staged, contrived, or digitally composited.
